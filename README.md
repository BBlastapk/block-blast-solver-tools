## Puzzle Solver Strategies

Strong Block Blast sessions rely more on board control than random placement.

Key concepts:

- Keep at least two columns open
- Avoid stacking uneven shapes near corners
- Delay large square placements until mid-board stabilizes
- Use combo chains instead of emergency clears
- Protect center-board flexibility

## Common Gameplay Mistakes

### Filling the Center Too Early

Most failed runs happen because players block center flexibility before preparing edge recovery space.

### Using Revives Too Early

Revives are strongest when recovering from forced placements, not minor mistakes.

### Overusing Speed Modes

Fast gameplay reduces placement accuracy during high-density boards.

## Touch Response and Placement Consistency

One overlooked factor in puzzle gameplay performance is touch responsiveness during long sessions.

Even small delays caused by:
- background applications
- battery optimization
- thermal throttling
- low storage space

can affect placement accuracy during crowded board states.

Players using larger screens or emulator setups often notice improved pattern recognition because additional screen space makes future placements easier to visualize.
